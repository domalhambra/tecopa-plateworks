Getting your tracks into TrailPrint

TrailPrint accepts GPX, KML, and KMZ files — and one file can carry many tracks, so the fastest path is always a single bulk export from wherever your tracks live today.

From Gaia GPS — export a whole folder as one file

  1. On gaiagps.com (the website, not the phone app), file the tracks you want into a folder — or use an existing trip or year folder.
  2. Open the folder from your Saved Items and choose Export → GPX.
  3. One download contains every track in the folder. Drop it here.

From onX (Backcountry / Hunt / Offroad) — multi-select in the Web Map

  1. Open the onX Web Map on a computer and click My Content.
  2. Click Select, tick the checkboxes next to the tracks or whole folders you want, then click Export.
  3. Choose GPX for tracks (tracks exported as KML become plain lines). onX colors and icons don't carry over — that's fine; you'll restyle here.

From Strava / Garmin / other watches

  • Strava: on an activity page, choose Export GPX; for everything at once, request your account archive (Settings → My Account → Download or Delete Your Account → Download Request) — it includes every activity file.
  • Garmin Connect: open an activity → gear menu → Export to GPX.
  • Most other platforms (COROS, Suunto, Komoot, Ride with GPS) have a per-activity GPX export; look for "Export" or "Download" on the activity page.

Tips for a better poster (and film)

  • Prefer recorded tracks over drawn routes. Recordings carry dates, and dates are what group your trips into journeys, drive the worn-path effect where you've returned often, and put the time-lapse film in the right order. Drawn routes have no date and reveal last in the film.
  • Drop everything at once or over many visits — tracks accumulate on the poster until you start over.
  • Re-dropping a file you already added is safe. TrailPrint recognizes files that already back the poster and skips them.

Continuing a poster next year (editions)

  1. Choose Continue a poster… and drop last year's TrailPrint PNG. The whole composition comes back — tracks, style, crop, markers, photos. (The poster is the save file: everything travels inside it.)
  2. Add this year's GPX and render. The new print becomes Edition 2, with its lineage in the cartouche.

Re-export the whole folder if it's easier. TrailPrint recognizes tracks already on the poster and skips them, so dropping a bundle that mixes last year's recordings with this year's only adds the new ones — a recording counts once, no matter how many times you export it. (Walking the same trail again on a different day is a new journey, and still earns its worn-path weight.)

Privacy and reprints

  • Finished PNGs embed their own recipe, so any TrailPrint file can be reprinted or continued forever — no account, no cloud, nothing to lose.
  • Sharing a copy? Turn off Reprintable file at the proof step to strip your exact route coordinates from the shared image.
