Getting your tracks into Tecopa Printworks

Tecopa Printworks accepts GPX, KML, and KMZ files — and one file can carry many tracks, so the fastest path is always a single bulk export from wherever your tracks live today.

Around the studio

The left rail moves you through the work, in order:

  • Library — pick a region and drop your tracks, or reopen a poster PNG to reprint it or add next year's edition.
  • Compose — frame the poster (drag on the map; arrow keys nudge it), name places, set the print size or a device/social size.
  • Style · Layers · Light — render a proof, then tune the route, terrain, named geography, elevation profile and Journey Light. With Live proof on, each edit re-proofs itself; presets give you a look in one click.
  • Social — repurpose the accepted poster for Reels, feed posts and stories (with the caption/action safe zones drawn in), write a caption, and build a share kit; or stage it as photographed wall art.
  • Films — the journeys inking themselves onto the poster, as an archival APNG or a WebP/MP4 to post.
  • Exports — every render this session, with re-download.

Press ? for the full shortcut list, ⌘K (or Ctrl+K) to search every option and action, and 1–8 to jump between sections.

From Gaia GPS — export a whole folder as one file

  1. On gaiagps.com (the website, not the phone app), file the tracks you want into a folder — or use an existing trip or year folder.
  2. Open the folder from your Saved Items and choose Export → GPX.
  3. One download contains every track in the folder. Drop it here.

From onX (Backcountry / Hunt / Offroad) — multi-select in the Web Map

  1. Open the onX Web Map on a computer and click My Content.
  2. Click Select, tick the checkboxes next to the tracks or whole folders you want, then click Export.
  3. Choose GPX for tracks (tracks exported as KML become plain lines). onX colors and icons don't carry over — that's fine; you'll restyle here.

From Strava / Garmin / other watches

  • Strava: on an activity page, choose Export GPX; for everything at once, request your account archive (Settings → My Account → Download or Delete Your Account → Download Request) — it includes every activity file.
  • Garmin Connect: open an activity → gear menu → Export to GPX.
  • Most other platforms (COROS, Suunto, Komoot, Ride with GPS) have a per-activity GPX export; look for "Export" or "Download" on the activity page.

Tips for a better poster (and film)

  • Prefer recorded tracks over drawn routes. Recordings carry dates, and dates are what group your trips into journeys, drive the worn-path effect where you've returned often, and put the time-lapse film in the right order. Drawn routes have no date and reveal last in the film.
  • Drop everything at once or over many visits — tracks accumulate on the poster until you start over.
  • Re-dropping a file you already added is safe. Tecopa Printworks recognizes files that already back the poster and skips them.

Continuing a poster next year (editions)

  1. In Library, choose Open a poster… (or drag the PNG anywhere) and pick Add this year. The whole composition comes back — tracks, style, crop, markers, photos. (The poster is the save file: everything travels inside it.)
  2. Add this year's GPX and render. The new print becomes Edition 2, with its lineage in the cartouche.

Re-export the whole folder if it's easier. Tecopa Printworks recognizes tracks already on the poster and skips them, so dropping a bundle that mixes last year's recordings with this year's only adds the new ones — a recording counts once, no matter how many times you export it. (Walking the same trail again on a different day is a new journey, and still earns its worn-path weight.)

Privacy and reprints

  • Finished PNGs embed their own recipe, so any Tecopa Printworks file can be reprinted or continued forever — no account, no cloud, nothing to lose.
  • Sharing a copy? Turn off Reprintable file in the inspector (or flip the Share copy switch in the Social studio) to strip your exact route coordinates from the shared image.
