Why you can trust the file

The proof is the print

The low-res proof you approve and the full-resolution print are the same composition, painted twice — pixel-for-pixel, by design.

Reprint forever

A poster you print today re-renders from its own file after any future upgrade. The v1 recipe is frozen and tested against every release.

Private by default

Questions

Why only a few regions?

Each plate is built from real 3DEP elevation data, and we refuse to print terrain we can't back with real ground. Adding a region is deliberate craft, not a checkbox — and the request list decides what's cut next.

What if TrailPrint disappears?

Your poster reprints itself. The full recipe — composition, route geometry, source hashes, and embedded photos — lives inside the PNG, so any TrailPrint file re-renders at print resolution from the file alone. No account, no server, no lock-in.

How do I get my tracks in?

Export GPX from Gaia, onX, Strava, or your watch — one bulk file can carry many tracks. Re-export your whole folder every year; a recording counts once, and a repeat visit on a new day still earns its worn-path weight.

What makes the time-lapse work?

Your tracks already carry dates, and rendering is deterministic, so a film isn't a new kind of output — it's one poster painted many times, each frame a prefix of your journeys in day order. The final frame is byte-identical to the still print.
